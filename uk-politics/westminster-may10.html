Westminster: 10 May 2026

May 2026

In Parliament

Parliament is prorogued. The House of Commons next sits on Wednesday 13 May for the State Opening of Parliament. The Yeoman of the Guard will carry out the ceremonial search of the cellars at 9:30am; the King's Speech follows. The first day of debate on the Speech — with the Leader of the Opposition and the Prime Minister both scheduled to contribute — begins the same afternoon. Pre-briefed headline bills include the abolition of NHS England, a Railways Bill establishing Great British Railways, an AI Growth Lab, and legislation providing a mechanism for dynamic alignment with EU law in food standards, carbon trading, and electricity.

Government

Starmer has confirmed he will not resign following Thursday's results and is preparing to use the King's Speech as a legislative reset. ITV News reported he will make a major speech on Monday ahead of the State Opening to frame the government's programme. Gordon Brown and Harriet Harman — the former Deputy Leader — have been brought in as senior advisors, per the same reporting; the Downing Street announcements of their precise roles are expected Monday.

The Parties

Thursday's English local elections — England only; no Scottish Parliament or Senedd contests were on this cycle — produced results of unusual severity across all the major parties. With all 136 councils declared, Reform UK gained a net 1,451 seats and took control of 14 councils, including Essex County Council, Suffolk, and Havering, its first London borough. Labour lost a net 1,496 seats and 38 councils. The Conservatives lost a net 563 seats and 6 councils, though held Bexley, Westminster, and Wandsworth. The Liberal Democrats gained 1 council; the Greens gained 4. On the national equivalent vote as calculated by Professor John Curtice, Reform took 26–27%, with Labour, the Conservatives, Greens, and Liberal Democrats clustered between 14% and 20%.

The pressure on Starmer's leadership is active. As of Saturday afternoon, 37 Labour MPs have publicly called for his resignation, with ten doing so in the 24 hours following the results. Labour MP Catherine West has indicated she intends to put herself forward as a stalking horse candidate on Monday. Supporters of Greater Manchester Mayor Andy Burnham have urged MPs to hold off, per GB News. Work and Pensions Secretary Liz Kendall told the BBC the Prime Minister "is not going to go, and he's not going to set a timetable." Kemi Badenoch, facing her own set of losses — including Essex County Council, her own local authority — declared the Conservatives "coming back" and said the results were "the beginning, not the end." Nigel Farage described the outcome as an "historic shift in politics."

What to Watch

Monday 11 May: Starmer's reset speech; Catherine West's stalking horse announcement. Whether the number of Labour MPs calling for resignation crosses 40 — the threshold that typically triggers a formal no-confidence process — is the immediate signal.

Wednesday 13 May: State Opening and King's Speech. The legislative programme will be read against the election results: whether the government has recalibrated toward the voters it lost to Reform and the Greens, or maintained its existing course.

Thursday 14 May: Home Affairs Committee evidence session on antisemitism in the UK.
