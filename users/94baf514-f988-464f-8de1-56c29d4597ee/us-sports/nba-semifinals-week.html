NBA Semifinals: Knicks and Thunder Sweep In; Spurs Take 3-2 Lead

May 2026 · Published 13 May 2026, 12:16 AM EST

Eastern Conference

The No. 2 New York Knicks completed a 4-0 sweep of the No. 7 Philadelphia 76ers with a 144-114 Game 4 win in Philadelphia on May 10, the franchise's first best-of-seven sweep since the 1999 East semis against Atlanta. New York hit 25 three-pointers on 44 attempts, tying the NBA single-game playoff record for makes. Deuce McBride, starting for the injured OG Anunoby, went 4-for-4 from deep in a first quarter in which the Knicks made 11 three-pointers — also a playoff record for a single period. Jalen Brunson and Josh Hart led the floor; the Knicks advance to a second straight Eastern Conference Finals, awaiting the Cleveland–Detroit winner.

The No. 1 Cleveland Cavaliers and No. 6 Detroit Pistons are tied 2-2 heading into Game 5 on May 13, 8 p.m. ET on ESPN at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse. Donovan Mitchell scored 35 in a 112-103 Cavaliers win in Game 4, with Cleveland opening the second half on a 23-0 run that decided the game.

Western Conference

The No. 1 Oklahoma City Thunder swept the No. 4 Los Angeles Lakers 4-0, closing the series 115-110 on the road Monday night. With OKC's first-round sweep of Phoenix included, Mark Daigneault's team has won eight consecutive playoff games entering the conference finals — the longest streak by a No. 1 seed through two rounds since the 2017 Warriors.

The No. 2 San Antonio Spurs took a 3-2 series lead over the No. 6 Minnesota Timberwolves with a 126-97 home win on May 12 after Minnesota had levelled the series Sunday. Victor Wembanyama finished with 27 points and 17 rebounds in the Game 5 rout; Anthony Edwards was held to 18 on 7-of-22 shooting. Game 6 is in Minneapolis on May 14, with Game 7 — if necessary — back in San Antonio on May 16.

What to Watch

Cavs–Pistons Game 5: tonight, May 13, 8 p.m. ET, ESPN. Wolves–Spurs Game 6: May 14, Target Center. If the Cavs win, the East Finals could open as early as May 17. The Thunder, idle since Monday, will have rest as a structural advantage against either Spurs or Wolves; OKC's last competitive game was three days before the Western Conference Finals tipoff window opens.
