NBA Conference Finals Open: Thunder-Spurs Game 1, Cavs Advance to Face Knicks

May 2026 · Published 18 May 2026, 1:02 PM EST

The 2026 Western Conference Finals open Monday night at Paycom Center in Oklahoma City (8:30 p.m. ET, NBC/Peacock). The Oklahoma City Thunder enter at 10-0 — the best two-round playoff record in franchise history — having swept the first two rounds without a loss. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, who won his second consecutive NBA MVP award on Sunday, leads the Thunder into the matchup that has defined the Western bracket since November: SGA versus Victor Wembanyama.

The San Antonio Spurs reach the Conference Finals for the first time since 2005 after eliminating the Minnesota Timberwolves in six games, capped by a 139-109 road win in Game 6. Wembanyama — the unanimous Defensive Player of the Year and the youngest ever to claim the award — recorded 19 points, 6 rebounds, and 3 blocks in the closeout. He is the central figure in the most anticipated individual matchup of the postseason.

Sunday night's Game 7 in Detroit was decisive: the Cleveland Cavaliers defeated the Pistons 125-94, ending Detroit's breakthrough playoff run. Jarrett Allen scored 23 points; Sam Merrill added 23; Evan Mobley contributed 21 points and 12 rebounds. The margin — 31 points — reflected Cleveland's ability to lock in when elimination arrived.

The Cavaliers now face the New York Knicks in the Eastern Conference Finals, Game 1 on Tuesday (8 p.m. ET, ESPN). The Knicks have been dominant through two rounds: a 4-2 first-round win over Atlanta, then a sweep of Philadelphia in which New York outscored the Sixers by an average of 19.4 points — the best differential through two rounds in the 43-year history of the 16-team playoff format. The Knicks have won 10 straight playoff games and are pursuing the franchise's first championship since 1973.

  • West Game 1: Spurs at Thunder — Monday, May 18, 8:30 p.m. ET (NBC/Peacock)
  • East Game 1: Cavaliers at Knicks — Tuesday, May 19, 8 p.m. ET (ESPN)
  • West Game 2: Spurs at Thunder — Wednesday, May 20 (TNT)
  • East Game 2: Cavaliers at Knicks — Thursday, May 21 (ESPN)
