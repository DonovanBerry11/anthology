Starmer's Survival: King's Speech Delivered as Health Secretary Resigns

May 2026 · Published 15 May 2026, 2:36 PM EST

The Legislative Agenda

The State Opening of Parliament on May 13 set out 35 bills covering defence, housing, Lords reform, and digital identity. The two headline measures are the European Partnership Bill — a framework for adopting EU rules as Brussels deals are struck, intended to ease the regulatory friction added by Brexit — and the Hillsborough Law, formally the Public Office (Accountability) Bill, which introduces a statutory duty of candour for public servants and fulfils a commitment made after repeated deferrals by previous governments. Other bills include a Social Housing Renewal Bill, Commonhold and Leasehold Reform, new Lords powers to remove peers, and a digital ID framework. BritBrief's coverage characterised it as the most substantial legislative programme the government has tabled since taking office.

The Political Context

The agenda landed into a government under acute political pressure. The May 8 local elections produced Labour's worst results in a generation: Reform UK won approximately 27% of the national equivalent vote to Labour's 15%, based on Sky News' projected national share calculation. Reform made gains of roughly 1,454 net council seats on media tallies, taking seats Labour had held for decades in areas including Wigan and Tameside. As of May 14, approximately 92 Labour MPs had publicly called on Starmer to set a departure date, according to LabourList's running tracker — above the 81-MP threshold required to formally trigger a leadership contest.

Streeting's Resignation

The most consequential development of May 14 was Health Secretary Wes Streeting's resignation from cabinet. Streeting told Starmer in his resignation letter that "it is now clear you will not lead the Labour Party into the next general election" — and confirmed to allies, according to CNN, that he holds more than 81 nominations for a formal leadership challenge, though as of May 14 he had not filed them. Manchester Mayor Andy Burnham has also signalled readiness to stand; ITV News reported that Downing Street has confirmed it will not block Burnham returning to the Commons via a by-election. More than 100 MPs have signed a statement defending Starmer, and he told cabinet on May 13 he regards his programme as a "ten-year project of renewal."

What to Watch

Whether Streeting files his 81 nominations — the step that formally activates the leadership contest mechanism — within days or weeks. Lords debates on the King's Speech run May 14–21. CNBC noted on May 13 that gilt markets remain sensitive to political instability, with sterling and gilt yields tracked closely by investors watching for signs of prolonged leadership uncertainty.
