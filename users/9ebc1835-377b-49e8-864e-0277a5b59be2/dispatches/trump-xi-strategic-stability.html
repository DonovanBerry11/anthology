Trump-Xi summit closes with three-year 'strategic stability' framework but few specifics

May 2026 · Published 18 May 2026, 12:33 AM EST

Trump's May 13–15 state visit to Beijing closed Friday with a joint commitment to "constructive strategic stability" as the frame for US–China relations "over the next three years and beyond" — but with little of the substance it is meant to organise actually settled.

Trump told reporters China had agreed to buy 200 Boeing aircraft, plus US soybeans, oil and LNG; Beijing did not confirm those figures, listing only trade and investment councils and an "in-principle" tariff cut on equivalent product baskets. Marco Rubio said US Taiwan policy remained "unchanged." No agreement was reached on AI chip export controls or rare earths beyond a protocol on non-state actors' access to AI models. China's Iran undertaking — support for reopening the Strait of Hormuz — was general, not operational.

Trump invited Xi to Washington in late September, his first US visit in over a decade. That meeting inherits the unresolved agenda.
