Global Bond Rout Deepens as JGBs, Treasuries and Bunds Sell Off in Lockstep

May 2026 · Published 19 May 2026, 12:22 AM EST

Long-dated government bond yields rose in lockstep across Tokyo, London, New York and Frankfurt on Monday as Brent crude pushed above $111 a barrel earlier in the session on the Hormuz closure, before retracing on US sanctions-waiver reports. Japan's 30-year JGB yield reached a record near 4.20 percent, the highest since the tenor was first issued in 1999; the 10-year hit 2.78 percent, a new 29-year high.

In the US, the 10-year Treasury touched 4.6 percent and the 30-year cleared 5 percent at auction on May 13 for the first time since 2007. UK 10-year gilts hit their highest since 2008; Germany's Bund reached 3.19 percent, a 15-year high. Treasury earlier this month revised its Q2 borrowing estimate to $189bn, $79bn above the February projection. The next FOMC meets June 16–17, the first under chair Kevin Warsh.
