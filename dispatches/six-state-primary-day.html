Six-State Primary Day

May 2026 · Published 19 May 2026, 12:37 AM EST

Voters in Alabama, Georgia, Idaho, Kentucky, Oregon and Pennsylvania go to the polls Tuesday, May 19, in the first multi-state primary day since Louisiana v. Callais. In Pennsylvania, Governor Josh Shapiro has endorsed Bob Harvie in PA-1 against Brian Fitzpatrick, firefighter union president Bob Brooks in a four-way PA-7 primary, and 2024 nominee Janelle Stelson in PA-10 against Scott Perry. Georgia's Republican Senate primary — Buddy Carter, Mike Collins, Kemp-backed Derek Dooley, plus two others — has no Trump endorsement, with a June 16 runoff if no one clears 50 percent; Jon Ossoff is unopposed. Senator Jim Risch and Governor Brad Little face crowded but under-funded Idaho fields. In KY-4, Thomas Massie trails Trump-endorsed Ed Gallrein 53-45 in a Quantus Insights poll. Polls close 6 p.m. local in Kentucky, 7 p.m. in Alabama and Georgia, 8 p.m. in Idaho and Pennsylvania; Oregon ballots due by 8 p.m.
