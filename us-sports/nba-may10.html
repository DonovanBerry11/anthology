NBA: The Guy Nobody Counted On

May 2026 · Published 10 May 2026, 3:54 AM EST

Lead Story

Ajay Mitchell barely played in last year's playoffs. The Belgian-born second-round pick spent Oklahoma City's championship run as a spectator — 23 years old, deep on the bench, watching. On Friday night at Crypto.com Arena, he scored 24 points and dished 10 assists without a single turnover, and the Thunder blew out the Lakers 131-108 to take a 3-0 series lead.

Mitchell's line put him in genuinely rare company. He became only the fourth player since 1990 — alongside Magic Johnson, Chris Paul, and Tyrese Haliburton — to record 20+ points, 10+ assists, and 3+ steals without a turnover in a playoff game. He's now starting in place of the injured Jalen Williams, which was supposed to be the caveat on Oklahoma City's title defence. It has not functioned as one.

The Thunder are 7-0 this postseason. They have won by a combined 59 points in this series alone. They are one win from their second consecutive Conference Finals appearance, and they are doing it with a player no one expected to matter.

Around the League

The Knicks hold a 3-0 lead over the 76ers after Friday's 108-94 win in Philadelphia. Jalen Brunson had 33 points and is averaging 31.3 per game for the series. Philadelphia has lost the last two by a combined 28 points. The Knicks can sweep on Sunday.

Victor Wembanyama delivered one of the more ridiculous lines of the playoffs: 39 points, 15 rebounds, and 5-plus blocks in San Antonio's 115-108 Game 3 win over Minnesota. He's the fourth player in NBA history with 35+ points, 15+ rebounds, and 5+ blocks in a playoff game. Anthony Edwards matched him with 32 points and 14 rebounds — this series is going the distance. Spurs lead 2-1.

The Pistons lead the Cavaliers 2-1 after Cleveland's 116-109 win in Game 3. Donovan Mitchell had 35 points and 10 rebounds; James Harden, 35 years old, hit the dagger three with 25.9 seconds left. Game 4 is Monday.
