NBA Conference Semifinals — 11 May 2026

May 2026 · Published 12 May 2026, 12:18 AM EST

East Semifinals: Knicks Sweep

The Knicks finished a 4-0 sweep of the 76ers on Saturday, May 10, with a 144-114 win in Philadelphia. New York set an NBA postseason record with 11 first-quarter three-pointers and tied the postseason record at 25 made threes for the game. Deuce McBride, starting for the injured OG Anunoby, hit seven threes (4-for-4 in the first quarter) and scored 25 points; Jalen Brunson added 22, Josh Hart and Karl-Anthony Towns 17 apiece. It is New York's first sweep in a best-of-seven series since 1999. The Knicks return to the Eastern Conference Finals for a second straight year and will play the winner of Cavaliers–Pistons.

East Semifinals: Cavaliers Tie Series

Cleveland evened its semifinal with Detroit on Monday, May 11, beating the Pistons 112-103 after trailing 0-2. Donovan Mitchell finished with 43 points, including 39 in the second half — tying the NBA postseason record for points in a half. James Harden added 24 points, 11 assists, and 4 steals. The series is tied 2-2, with Game 5 in Cleveland later this week.

West Semifinals: Thunder Pursue Sweep

The defending champion Thunder entered Monday's Game 4 in Los Angeles up 3-0 and unbeaten through seven playoff games, having won every postseason game by at least 18 points. The series margin sits at 19.6 points per game on average. Ajay Mitchell, starting for an injured rotation guard, posted career playoff highs of 24 points and 10 assists in OKC's 131-108 Game 3 win on May 9. Game 4 in Los Angeles tipped off after 10:30pm ET on Monday; the result was not finalised at the time of filing. Teams with a 3-0 lead in a best-of-seven series are 162-0 all time.

West Semifinals: Spurs–Timberwolves Tied

San Antonio and Minnesota are level 2-2 entering Game 5 at the Frost Bank Center, scheduled for 8:00pm ET on Tuesday, May 12. Minnesota arrived as the No. 6 seed after dispatching the No. 3 Denver Nuggets in six games; San Antonio holds home-court advantage as the No. 2 seed.

What to Watch

Cavaliers–Pistons Game 5 (date TBC), Spurs–Timberwolves Game 5 (May 12, 8:00pm ET), and Thunder–Lakers Game 5 (May 13 if needed). The Eastern Conference Finals will open later in May once the Cavaliers–Pistons series concludes.
