NBA Semifinals: Cavs and Spurs Up 3-2; Game 6s Friday

May 2026 · Published 14 May 2026, 12:16 AM EST

East: Cavaliers Take Game 5 in OT, Lead 3-2

The Cleveland Cavaliers beat the Detroit Pistons 117-113 in overtime at Little Caesars Arena on Wednesday to take a 3-2 lead in the Eastern Conference Semifinals. Yahoo Sports' game summary records James Harden leading the Cavs with 30 points, 8 rebounds and 6 assists; Donovan Mitchell added 21 points, including seven in the overtime period. Evan Mobley finished with 19 points, 8 rebounds and 8 assists, and Max Strus contributed 20 off the bench. Cade Cunningham led the Pistons with a series-high 39 points, 9 assists and 7 rebounds in defeat. The end of regulation included a contested no-call on what Detroit argued was a foul on Cunningham; the league has not commented on the play.

Cleveland has now won three straight after Detroit took the first two games by ten points each. Game 6 is in Cleveland on Friday May 15 at 7:00 p.m. ET on Prime Video, per Yahoo Sports' schedule; a Game 7, if necessary, would return to Detroit on Sunday.

West: Spurs Lead Wolves 3-2 Ahead of Game 6

The San Antonio Spurs beat the Minnesota Timberwolves 126-97 at the Frost Bank Center on Tuesday May 12 to take a 3-2 series lead. Per NBA.com's recap, Victor Wembanyama posted 27 points, 17 rebounds and 5 assists in his first game back after a Game 4 ejection. The Spurs outscored Minnesota 68-36 in the paint and led by as many as 30. Game 6 is in Minneapolis on Friday May 15.

Conference Finals Field

Both Game 1 spots are already taken. The New York Knicks completed a 4-0 sweep of the Philadelphia 76ers, making this their second consecutive Eastern Conference Finals appearance; the Oklahoma City Thunder swept the Los Angeles Lakers 4-0 in the West. The Conference Finals schedule will be set when both semifinal series conclude; the league has not released specific dates pending the closeouts. The NBA Finals tip off June 3 on ABC.

What to Watch

Game 6, Pistons at Cavaliers, Friday May 15 at 7:00 p.m. ET — Cleveland's first chance to close. Game 6, Spurs at Timberwolves, Friday May 15 — San Antonio's first chance to close. If either series goes to seven, Game 7s would be Sunday May 17 at the higher seed.
